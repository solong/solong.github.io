Case Study: HatF2

Summary: HatF2 is a digital asset search engine capable of asset discovery, capture, alerting, and post-capture analysis. An elegant user interface allows for rapid search whilst a resilient crawler works diligently to populate the dataset.

Background

According to Juniper Research the current Video Game loot box market is estimated to be worth $30 billion worldwide.

Some of the assets derived from loot boxes are high value items ($1000 - $30,000) or form part of a broader collectable set.

With over 300 million items in existance as of 2016, searching for specific items is non-trivial; Traditional venues on trade exchanges are often noisy due to imprecise listings and the lack of integrated execution features, with trade servers dealing in low quality or worthless items.

HatF2 seeks to facilitate asset discovery in a more precise manner by accurately capturing raw asset data and generating a rich searchable domain layer derived from this data lake.

Technical Brief

Summary: (a) To index a given digital asset universe, allowing authenticated search on attributes both known and unknown at the time of capture. (b) to provide monitoring and alerting on detection of anomalous assets.

  • To discover and grow a set of digital assets stores
  • To capture assets contained within each digital asset store
  • To prioritize public facing digital asset stores
  • To provide authenticated search functionality on all assets
  • To provide monitoring and alerting for anomalous assets
  • To allow search accross multiple attributes simultaneously
  • To ensure search is performant at all times
  • To provide high durability of raw data
  • To provide insight into the administration of the service

Technologies

Java, Angular, MongoDB, AWS, Spring
